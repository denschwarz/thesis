\section*{Abstract}
The presented analysis aims at a measurement of the jet mass in collimated top quark decays, where the top quark is reconstructed within one single jet. The jet mass is sensitive to the top quark mass and therefore an essential jet substructure variable for top quark identification. Studies with various jet clustering algorithm are presented to find most suitable method of reconstruction. Consequently, the exclusive jet clustering algorithm XCone shows a great performance and is used to obtain a jet mass distribution that is unfolded to particle level. An unfolding is applied to be able to compare with theory calculations and measure a top quark mass without relying on Monte Carlo simulations. Proving the method of this analysis, an unfolded jet mass distribution is presented as result. The measurement is performed in the lepton+jets channel, which offers a good suppression of non-$t\bar{t}$ backgrounds while retaining a high selection efficiency and uses data recorded by the CMS detector at a centre-of-mass energy of 13 TeV.
